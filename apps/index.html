Free apps for SEND families

Simple visual support apps that install straight to your phone and work completely offline — ready in the supermarket, the car park or the waiting room, even with no signal. Every app is free, with no ads, no accounts and no tracking. Nothing you add ever leaves your device.

🔜

Now & Next

A visual Now/Next/Then board for children who cope best when they can see what's happening now and what comes next. Choose from nearly 80 built-in picture cards or add photos of real places and people — for many children, a photo of Grandma's actual front door communicates far better than any symbol. A child lock lets your child look and tap "Done" without rearranging the board.

Visual Timer

Time made visible: a coloured disc that shrinks as time passes, so children who can't yet read a clock can see exactly how long is left. The screen stays awake while it runs, the countdown can be locked so it can't be stopped mid-wait, numbers can be hidden for children who find digits stressful, and it ends with a gentle chime rather than a jarring alarm.

Choice Board

Offer two, three or four options as pictures and let your child tap to choose — "park or library?", "red cup or blue cup?". Choices reduce demand-anxiety, and making them visual makes them possible for children who struggle to hold spoken options in mind. The app can speak the choice out loud, works with photos of your child's real things, and locks so the options can't be changed mid-decision.

How to install the apps

These are installable web apps — nothing to download from an app store, no account to create. On your phone:

  • Open the app's address in your browser (for example timer.helpset.uk).
  • Android (Chrome): tap the menu (⋮) and choose Install app or Add to Home screen.
  • iPhone (Safari): tap the Share button and choose Add to Home Screen.

The app then opens from its own icon like any other app — full screen, instantly, and without needing the internet. Visit the app once while online and it's yours offline from then on.

Why offline matters

The moments these tools are needed most — a transition wobble in a supermarket queue, "two more minutes" at the park, a choice that needs making in a car park — are exactly the moments you can't rely on signal or wait for a page to load. Each app opens instantly and works with flight mode on. Everything your child sees, including any photos you add, is stored only on your own phone.

Free means free. No ads, no subscriptions, no "premium" versions, no data collection. Helpset is built by one parent who wanted these tools to exist. If they help your family and you'd like to support the work, there's a Ko-fi page — but the apps are complete as they are, for everyone.
