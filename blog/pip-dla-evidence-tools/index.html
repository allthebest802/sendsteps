Free Tools to Help You Record Evidence for a PIP or DLA Application

Applying for Personal Independence Payment (PIP) or Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for a child with autism, ADHD or additional needs requires detailed evidence of how their condition affects daily living. These free Helpset tools help you build that evidence — no login, no cost, ready to use today.

PIP and DLA — what's the difference?

Disability Living Allowance (DLA) is for children under 16 with a disability or health condition that means they need more help or supervision than a child of the same age without the condition.

Personal Independence Payment (PIP) is for people aged 16 and over. When a young person with SEND turns 16, they may need to transition from DLA to PIP — a separate application with its own assessment criteria.

Both benefits are based on how a person's condition affects their daily life — not on their diagnosis. A child with autism who needs very little support may receive less support than a child with a less severe diagnosis who needs a great deal of help. The evidence you provide about day-to-day impact is what determines the outcome.

💜 Important: This page provides general information only. For specific benefits advice contact Turn2Us (turn2us.org.uk), Citizens Advice, Contact (contact.org.uk) or your local welfare rights service — all offer free specialist advice to families of disabled children.

What do DLA and PIP assessors look for?

Assessors look at how a condition affects daily functioning across specific areas. For DLA (children), these include:

  • Personal care — washing, dressing, eating, toileting
  • Supervision needs — risk of harm to self or others
  • Mobility and getting around
  • Communication and social interaction
  • Night-time care and supervision

The crucial question is always: how much more help does this child need compared to a child of the same age without their condition? The more specific and detailed your evidence, the stronger your application.

The biggest mistake families make is describing their child's diagnosis rather than its impact. "My child has autism" is not evidence. "My child requires an adult present to manage any change in routine, has meltdowns lasting 45-90 minutes occurring 3-4 times per week, and cannot be left unsupervised due to risk of self-harm" — that is evidence.

The diary evidence that makes the difference

DLA and PIP decision makers consistently say that detailed diary evidence — records kept over weeks rather than a summary written at application time — is the most compelling supporting evidence they receive. Starting to record now, even if your application is months away, will significantly strengthen your case.

Helpset tools that help build DLA and PIP evidence

🔥 Meltdown & Incident Tracker — your most important tool

For many families, the frequency and severity of meltdowns or emotional dysregulation episodes is the clearest evidence of supervision needs and daily living impact. The Helpset Meltdown Tracker lets you log each incident with time, trigger, duration, what happened and what support was needed to recover.

Four to six weeks of detailed incident logs — showing frequency, duration and the level of adult support required — provides powerful evidence for both DLA supervision needs and PIP daily living components.

😴 Sleep Logger — night-time care evidence

Night-time supervision needs are a significant part of DLA assessments for children with autism and ADHD. If your child wakes frequently, needs supervision at night, or has disrupted sleep that requires parental intervention, documenting this over time provides clear evidence of night-time care needs.

The Sleep Logger lets you record bedtime, wake times, night wakings and what support was needed. A month of records showing consistent disruption is far more compelling than a parent's verbal account.

💊 Medication Tracker

Documenting medication, dosage, timing and any side effects provides medical context for your application. If your child requires prompting to take medication, or if medication has significant side effects that impact daily functioning, recording this is relevant evidence.

🔋 Battery / Energy Tracker

Tracking your child's energy and emotional regulation levels helps demonstrate the cumulative daily impact of their condition. How to present your Helpset records as evidence

When submitting your DLA or PIP application or requesting a mandatory reconsideration, include your Helpset records as supporting evidence. Tips for presenting them effectively:

  • Print or screenshot 4-8 weeks of records — the longer the period, the more compelling the pattern
  • Write a brief cover note for each set of records explaining what they show
  • Quantify where possible — "these records show an average of X meltdowns per week, lasting Y minutes each, requiring Z adult support to manage"
  • Connect to the DLA/PIP criteria — specifically reference supervision needs, night-time care, daily living activities
  • Keep copies of everything you send and note the dates

📋 Useful phrases for DLA/PIP applications

  • "As evidenced by the attached incident log, my child requires adult supervision at all times due to risk of..."
  • "The attached sleep records show that my child wakes on average X times per night requiring..."
  • "My child needs significantly more help than a typical child of the same age with all personal care activities because..."
  • "The attached records demonstrate that my child experiences X per week on average, each requiring..."

Getting help with your application

DLA and PIP forms are long and complex. You don't have to complete them alone. These free services can help:

  • Contact (contact.org.uk) — specialist charity for families of disabled children, with a helpline and benefits advice
  • Turn2Us (turn2us.org.uk) — benefits calculator and adviser finder
  • Citizens Advice — free benefits help in person or online
  • Cerebra (cerebra.org.uk) — free legal and benefits advice for families of children with brain conditions
  • Your local welfare rights service — many councils offer free benefits advice

🔄 If you're refused: Don't give up at the first refusal. DLA and PIP refusals are frequently overturned at mandatory reconsideration or tribunal. Contact Citizens Advice or a welfare rights adviser immediately if your application is refused.
